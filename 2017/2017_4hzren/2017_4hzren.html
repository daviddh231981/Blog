Renaissance Hotel, Huizhou

Mostly photo review of Marriott’s Renaissance in Huizhou. This was a night stay before traveling to Shenzhen for a meeting and within striking distance of Huizhou and visiting a few friends of mine.

I arrived the hotel in the afternoon… take an elevator upstairs. This is the only Marriott within striking distance of the city, I’m not interested in a 40-minute taxi ride.

Full decorations are in play for Christmas (admittedly it’s closer to New Year’s, but no difference).

It was a lengthy elevator ride to the 36th floor, then a short hallway walk.

The room I had included two beds…

And two chairs. We all know how solo travelers like rooms with two beds.

The bathroom is massive. There’s a shower room, and a toilet, both with doors.

Then there’s a bathtub if you want to relax.

Then water. With branding.

I am a morning person. Don’t ask how. But I have this problem, and it’s waking up early. Breakfast opens at 6:30 in the morning. No, I’m not old!

With decent seating.

And a decent spread.

But the sunrise though!

This is a decent hotel if you’re willing to cough up the price. Well, this is the only Marriott in town, unless you want a 40-minute drive. I would stay here again.
